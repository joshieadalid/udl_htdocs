Laboratory of Data Science Applications for Urban Phenomenon Analysis







Describing Mexico Valley through complex data integration

The impact of the urban phenomena has been long studied with a fragmented approach, without closely looking at the relationship between different contexts that characterize it, in data from sensor-based, government data sources, and social media (complex data). The research developed in this laboratory integrate methodological framework that analyses sensor data, combined with health data and social data narratives that reflect how different communities perceive a urban phenomenon in space and time; exploring how these different heterogeneous sources can be combined to better understand the impact of urban phenomenon at the large-city, local and social level in the Valley of Mexico. The main purpose is the integration of different large data sources by combining a series of methods: Natural Language Processing, data mining , and Geographical Information System capabilities that together provide a better understanding of the quantitative and qualitative patterns emerging at a different spatial scale and temporal granularity. Overall, this shows howcomplex data integration can provide a better understanding of the impact of a given urban phenomenon. The work of this laboratory is oriented to analyses the impact of air pollution, crime, health, and natural resources.
Dr. Roberto E. Zagal Flores

Research and data gathering


In December 2023, came into operation the Urban Data Island ESCOM IPN Its purpose is to capture air quality, meteorological and social data with a sustainable approach. It is also equipped to be an open educational space. The project is in charge of the ESCOM Urban Data Laboratory, where undergraduate students in ISC, IA, CD, postgraduate and UAEM collaborate.
